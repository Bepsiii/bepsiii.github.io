FiveM Server Launch Planner

An Interactive Guide to Releasing "Drug Wars"

Phase 1: Strategy & Compliance

A successful server is built on a solid foundation. This phase is about understanding the rules of the ecosystem and making the critical architectural decisions that will define your project's entire lifecycle. Neglecting this stage is the most common reason for server failure.

The Trinity of Compliance

Your server operates under a strict hierarchy of rules. A violation at any level can jeopardize the entire project. Understanding this framework is non-negotiable.

1. Rockstar Games EULA

Highest Authority: Prohibits unauthorized commercial use of GTA V intellectual property.

2. Cfx.re Platform License

Platform Operator: Mandates use of Tebex for all monetization and forbids illegal content.

3. Tebex AUP

Monetization Gateway: Granular rules prohibiting pay-to-win, loot boxes, and direct currency sales.

The "Fictionality Defense"

A theme like "Drug Wars" is permissible only if framed as clear, fictional entertainment. You must create a controlled environment through a comprehensive server rulebook to mitigate risk.

  • ✓ Prohibit Offensive RP: Zero tolerance for hate speech, harassment, or graphic themes.
  • ✓ Enforce High-Quality RP: Ban random killing (RDM) and vehicle deathmatching (VDM).
  • ✓ Establish Safe Zones: Create "Green Zones" like hospitals where violence is forbidden.

Phase 2: Build & Develop

This is where your vision becomes a reality. This phase covers the foundational technical choices and the selection of scripts that will create your server's core gameplay loop. Every decision here impacts performance, stability, and future development.

Framework Showdown: ESX vs. QBCore

Choosing a framework is the most critical technical decision you'll make. It locks you into a development ecosystem. This choice must be driven by which framework's available scripts best serve your "Drug Wars" concept.

Feature ESX Ecosystem QBCore Ecosystem
Installation Manual, more complex Streamlined via txAdmin
Community Massive but fragmented Large and centralized (Discord)
Gang/Territory Scripts Mature options like `bixbi_territories` Robust options like `qb-gangs`
Modernity Older, battle-tested architecture Newer, more optimized design

Recommendation: QBCore

For a new "Drug Wars" server, **QBCore is recommended**. Its streamlined installation, modern architecture, centralized support, and comprehensive scripts like `qb-gangs` provide a more efficient and sustainable development path. It offers a lower barrier to entry and makes troubleshooting more manageable for new developers.

Core Systems Toolkit

ox_inventory

Inventory Management

The gold standard. Slot-based, supports item metadata (e.g., drug purity), and essential for the illicit economy.

eco_crafting

Drug Production

A powerful, multi-framework script for creating complex, multi-stage drug production recipes.

qb-gangs

Turf Control (QBCore)

The ideal choice for QBCore. Allows in-game gang creation, stashes, and territory control for turf wars.

Phase 3: Launch & Monetize

With development underway, it's time to plan your go-to-market strategy and build a compliant commercial engine. This phase covers how to officially announce your server, attract players, and generate revenue without violating platform rules.

12-Week Launch Action Plan

Weeks 1-2: Pre-Production

Finalize compliance docs, server rules, and make the final ESX/QBCore framework decision.

Weeks 3-8: Core Development

Setup hosting and install core systems: inventory, crafting, and territories. Integrate them to create the gameplay loop.

Weeks 9-10: Monetization & Alpha Test

Create and integrate your Tebex webstore. Design compliant cosmetic/convenience packages. Run a closed alpha test.

Week 11-12: Go-to-Market

Establish Discord, begin streamer outreach, and prepare a high-quality release post for the Cfx.re forums.

Launch Day!

Publish your forum post and officially open the server to the public.

Phase 4: Grow & Retain

A successful launch is just the beginning. Long-term viability depends on your ability to foster a positive community, manage feedback effectively, and continuously improve the server. This creates a "Community Flywheel" that drives sustained growth.

The Community Flywheel

This positive feedback loop is the engine of long-term success. Investing in your community is a direct investment in your server's future.

  1. A well-managed community feels valued.
  2. Valued players provide high-quality feedback.
  3. Developers use feedback to improve the server.
  4. Updates show players their voices are heard.
  5. Players become more invested, providing more feedback.

Long-Term Operations Checklist

Establish a Discord Hub

Use Discord as the central point for rules, announcements, and patch notes. Use bots for automation.

Structure Feedback

Create dedicated channels like `#bug-reports` and `#suggestions` to keep feedback organized and actionable.

Monitor Performance

Regularly use `resmon 1` in-game to identify and optimize resource-heavy scripts before they cause lag.
